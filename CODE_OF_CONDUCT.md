# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in the llm-exe project a respectful experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Standards

Behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experience.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.
- Focusing on what is best for the project and the community.
- Showing empathy toward other community members.

Behavior that is not acceptable:

- Personal attacks, insulting comments, or sustained disruption of discussions.
- Public or private harassment of any kind.
- Trolling, deliberate intimidation, or stalking.
- Publishing other people's private information without explicit permission.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or attention in project spaces.
- Other conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces (issues, pull requests, discussions, code review, the documentation site, and any synchronous chat the project may host) and applies when an individual is representing the project in public.

## Enforcement

Reports of unacceptable behavior should be sent to the maintainers at **help@llm-exe.com**. All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

### Enforcement ladder

1. **Correction.** A private written warning, including the reason and an expectation of changed behavior.
2. **Warning.** A formal warning. Continued violations may result in a temporary ban.
3. **Temporary ban.** A defined period of time during which the individual may not participate in project spaces.
4. **Permanent ban.** Removal from all project spaces, with no path to return.

The ladder is a default. Severe violations may skip steps.
